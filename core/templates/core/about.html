About Eco Kochi

"What light through yonder window breaks? 'Tis the dawn of a greener Kochi, where nature and mankind dance in sweet harmony."

Our Noble Quest

In this fair city of waters and ancient trade, we bear the torch of environmental stewardship. Like valiant knights of old, we have pledged ourselves to a cause most virtuous—to restore the natural glory of Kochi and preserve it for generations yet unborn.

The Tale of Our Beginning

In the year two thousand and fifteen, when pollution's dark clouds gathered o'er our beloved shores, a fellowship of kindred spirits convened. "Friends, Kochites, countrymen, lend me your ears," declared our founder, as visions of verdant canopies and crystalline waters danced in our collective imagination.

"The earth hath bubbles, as the water has, and these are of them. Let us protect what heaven hath bestowed upon our fair city."

Our Sacred Principles

To thine own self be true, and it must follow, as the night the day, thou canst not then be false to any man—nor to the earth that sustains thee. We abide by principles most sacred:

  • To preserve the natural beauty of Kochi's waterways
  • To cleanse the air of foul vapors that dim our azure skies
  • To propagate knowledge of sustainable living
  • To cultivate gardens where once stood concrete monuments
  • To champion the cause of renewable energy

Our Valiant Endeavors

What dreams may come when we have shuffled off this mortal coil? Dreams of a Kochi reborn, where the backwaters shine like burnished silver and mangroves stand sentinel against the rising tides.

Waterway Restoration

"Full fathom five thy plastic lies." Our volunteers dive deep to retrieve man's discarded follies from the briny deep.

Urban Forest Initiative

"I know a bank where the wild thyme blows." Within our concrete jungle, we nurture pockets of verdant wonder.

Sustainable Marketplace

Our marketplace supports craftsmen who honor the earth with their methods and tread lightly upon the ground.

Join Our Noble Company

"Once more unto the breach, dear friends, once more!" The battle for Kochi's environmental future calls for stalwart hearts and willing hands.

Whether thou art scholar or laborer, merchant or artist, there exists a place for thee in our fellowship. For as the Bard himself proclaimed, "We few, we happy few, we band of brothers" shall shape the destiny of our beloved home.
